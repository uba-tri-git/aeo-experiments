# GEO Tooling Will Commoditize: MarTech Consolidation Precedent

**Author:** Uba Alintah, VP Growth and GTM at Contently
**Date:** April 23, 2026
**Status:** Secondary-source evidence for SPOV 6 (GEO tooling commoditizes within 18 months)

## Thesis

The GEO/AEO tooling category (Peec AI, Scrunch, Otterly, AthenaHQ, Daydream, Profound) will follow the 2012-2018 MarTech consolidation pattern: strategic acquirers absorb category leaders, and independent vendors run out of landing spots within 18-36 months. Pricing in the tooling layer compresses to near-zero as the function is absorbed into enterprise suites. Consultants and agencies who price against proprietary tooling are pricing against a deflating asset.

## The MarTech consolidation precedent (documented)

### Email marketing — commoditized by 2013, per incumbent admission

Oracle acquired Eloqua in December 2012 for $871M (approximately 8x trailing revenue). Salesforce acquired ExactTarget in June 2013 for $2.5B ($33.75/share, 53% overnight premium, 8.3x trailing revenue). The sequence of acquisitions is documented in contemporaneous trade press coverage at CMSWire and Demand Gen Report from June 2013.

The most important quantitative evidence is a direct commoditization admission from an incumbent category leader at the time of the Salesforce-ExactTarget deal. Phil Fernandez, then CEO of Marketo (the largest remaining independent marketing automation vendor), told CMSWire in June 2013:

> "Marketo is leading digital marketing while Salesforce.com is buying 'commodity' capabilities with its $2.5 billion ExactTarget deal. [Email delivery is] getting commoditized, with prices falling from about $2 per thousand messages to 25 cents per thousand messages in recent years."

Source: https://www.cmswire.com/cms/customer-experience/salesforce-exacttarget-deal-reactions-the-day-after-021190.php

**The documented price compression is 87.5% on email send pricing** (from $2.00 to $0.25 per thousand messages) across the acquisition cycle. This is not a projection; this is Fernandez quoting observed price history at the time of the largest deal in the category.

### Marketing automation — independent vendors ran out of exits by 2018

Adobe acquired Marketo on September 20, 2018 for $4.75B, closed October 31, 2018. Vista Equity Partners had purchased Marketo for $1.8B in May 2016, producing a 2.64x return ($2.95B gain) in 27 months.

Forrester's principal analyst Rusty Warner, quoted in Marketing Tech News on the announcement:

> "This acquisition fundamentally resets the competitive playing field. There is not much room for an independent platform to compete as a best-of-breed point solution — no matter how important the breed or how best the solution. Frankly, Marketo was running out of potential landing spots when it came time for it to exit Vista, as the major marketing cloud vendors filled their portfolio gaps in other ways."

Source: https://www.marketingtechnews.net/news/adobes-purchase-marketos-b2b-martech-resets-playing-field/

IDC's Sales Technology Research Director Gerry Murray and Program VP Melissa Webster, writing in an IDC blog post the day of the announcement:

> "This deal solidifies Adobe's position as the market leader in marketing campaign management. The conversation is over."

Source: https://martech.org/with-marketo-mega-deal-adobes-reach-just-got-a-whole-lot-wider/

**The named-analyst assessment is that by September 2018, independent best-of-breed marketing automation had no defensible standalone future at the enterprise tier.** This is a Forrester position, not a speculative claim.

### The consolidation timeline (documented)

| Date | Deal | Price | Multiple | Category impact |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Dec 2012 | Oracle → Eloqua | $871M | ~8x | B2B marketing automation absorbed into Oracle Marketing Cloud |
| Jun 2013 | Salesforce → ExactTarget | $2.5B | 8.3x | Email delivery explicitly commoditized; prices fell 87.5% per Fernandez |
| May 2016 | Vista (PE) → Marketo | $1.8B | 6x | Last independent leader goes private |
| Sep 2018 | Adobe → Marketo | $4.75B | 10x+ | "Conversation is over" (IDC); no landing spots left for independents |

**Total elapsed time from first category-defining acquisition (Eloqua, Dec 2012) to "no independent landing spots" (Marketo, Sep 2018): approximately 69 months.** The acute commoditization phase — when Fernandez was already publicly calling the core function a commodity — occurred within 6 months of the first major category acquisition.

## Why GEO tooling follows the same pattern

The GEO tooling category today exhibits the same structural signals that preceded the MarTech consolidation:

1. **Strategic acquirer has entered.** Adobe announced the $1.9B Semrush acquisition explicitly for GEO capability (October 2025). Adobe is the same acquirer that consolidated the MarTech category with the Magento purchase (May 2018, $1.6B) and the Marketo purchase (September 2018, $4.75B). The playbook is literally the same company executing it again.

2. **Well-funded pure-plays are selling commodity analytics.** Peec AI raised a $21M Series A two days before the Adobe/Semrush announcement. Multiple other vendors (Scrunch, Otterly, AthenaHQ, Daydream, Profound) offer near-identical share-of-voice dashboards on top of LLM API calls. The underlying data access (LLM responses with web retrieval) is not proprietary — any vendor can build it. The Fernandez framing applies: these are commodity capabilities dressed as a category.

3. **Enterprise procurement preference compounds the effect.** Contently's experience across financial services and healthcare clients: enterprise security review for a new vendor takes 6-18 months. Adding an incumbent module (Adobe Experience Cloud) to an existing vendor contract takes weeks. The procurement friction asymmetry tilts spending toward incumbents the moment they have feature parity.

The combined pattern — strategic incumbent acquisition, commodity pure-play funding, enterprise procurement preference — produced 87.5% price compression in email delivery within 6 months of the first major category acquisition, and "no independent landing spots" within 69 months. Applied to GEO tooling, where the first major acquisition (Adobe/Semrush) has already occurred, the 18-month timeline for margin compression is conservative relative to the email-delivery precedent.

## What this means for consultants and agencies

The claim being tested is not "tooling goes away." The claim is that pricing consulting work against tool licensing is pricing against a deflating asset. Empirical evidence from the 2012-2018 MarTech cycle:

- Email marketing price per thousand messages fell from $2.00 to $0.25 (documented by Fernandez, CMSWire, June 2013)
- Marketo's standalone valuation required PE sponsorship to survive 27 months before a suite acquirer took it out (documented by TechCrunch and SaaStr, September 2018)
- Forrester's 2018 position: no room for independent best-of-breed point solutions at the enterprise tier

Consultants whose engagement model relies on a tool they resell, a tool they built, or a tool whose licensing they mark up are exposed to the same deflation. Consultants whose engagement model relies on methodology (controlled testing, matched-pair experiments, primary research — the SPOV 1 evidence pattern) are not, because methodology is not a commodity that acquirers can absorb into a suite.
